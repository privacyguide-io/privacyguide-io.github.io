Stop the Calls & Texts

Your phone number lives on data broker sites — that's where telemarketers buy their lists. Here's how to fight back on all three fronts, step by step.

1
National Do Not Call Registry
🇺🇸 Register Your Number with the FTC Free 5 min

The federal Do Not Call Registry legally requires telemarketers to stop calling you within 31 days of registration. This is the single most effective action you can take — and it's free.

1
Tap "Register / Verify" below — you'll land on a page with two buttons
2
Choose which button applies to you:
🆕
First time registering → tap "Register Here" → enter your phone number and an email address → tap Submit
🔍
Not sure if you're already registered → tap "Verify Here" → enter your number to check your status instantly
3
If you registered online, check your email for a confirmation from the FTC and click the link — without this, your registration won't go through
4
Wait up to 31 days for sales calls to stop. Registrations never expire — you only need to do this once.
Register / Verify → 📞 1-888-382-1222
⚠️ If the website shows an error, use the phone line instead — it's the same federal registry and works 24/7.
📌 The DNC Registry stops legal telemarketers. Scammers, political organizations, and surveys are exempt — use Steps 2 and 3 below for those.
📢 Already Registered But Still Getting Calls?

If your number has been on the registry for more than 31 days and you're still receiving sales calls, you can report the violation directly to the FTC — they use these reports to take legal action.

1
Tap "Report a Violation" below
2
Enter your phone number, the caller's number, the date it happened, and what the call was about
3
Your report goes into a shared law enforcement database used by the FTC, FCC, and state attorneys general to pursue violators
Report a Violation →
2
Stop Spam Texts
📱 SMS Opt-Out (TCPA)

Under federal TCPA law, any marketing text you receive must honor a STOP reply — this works even if you never signed up for their messages.

1
Open the unwanted text and reply with just the word STOP. You should get an automated confirmation message back.
2
For spam texts from senders who ignore STOP — forward the message to 7726 (spells SPAM). Your carrier will investigate and block that sender.
3
For persistent violations, file a complaint at consumercomplaints.fcc.gov — this escalates to federal enforcement
File FCC Complaint →

📊 How Status Tracking Works

Status is 100% manual — the user taps the status button on each broker's detail screen. There is no automation, no emails checked, no APIs. It's a self-reported tracker.

⭐ To DoDefault for all brokers. Not started yet.
🔄 In ProgressUser submitted a form or sent an email.
⏳ Awaiting ReplyWaiting on broker to respond / confirm removal.
✓ DoneRemoval confirmed. Shown with strikethrough on dashboard.

Each tap calls setStatus(brokerId, value) which immediately writes to localStorage. Changes persist across browser restarts with no network needed.

🚀 How to Deploy an Update (GitHub Pages)

The entire app is a single HTML file hosted on GitHub Pages. To push an update:
